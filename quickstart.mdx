## Create your first movie

This guide will walk you through recording your first scene and transforming it into an animated movie. You'll set up a fixed camera, record toy play, describe your story, and see the magic happen.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up your fixed camera">
    Position your camera so it has a clear view of your play area. The camera should be:
    
    - **Stationary** — mount it on a tripod or stable surface, don't hold it
    - **Well-lit** — ensure good lighting on your play area
    - **Framed properly** — leave enough space for toys to move around
    
    <Note>
      The fixed camera setup is crucial. Since the camera doesn't move, Cinemachine can cleanly separate toys from the background and your hands.
    </Note>
    
    Before recording, capture a **reference frame** of the empty scene (no toys, no hands). This helps the system understand what to remove later.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Record your first scene">
    Now for the fun part—playtime! Here's how to record:
    
    - **Hold your toy** and move it naturally as you play
    - **Speak the dialogue** as if you're the character
    - **Tell the story** through movement and voice
    - **Don't worry about your hands** appearing in frame—Cinemachine will handle that
    
    **Example:** Hold your dinosaur toy and say, "I'm searching for the lost treasure in the ancient forest. I can hear something moving in the trees!"
    
    <Info>
      Keep scenes short initially (30-60 seconds). You can create longer movies by combining multiple scenes later.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Describe your story to the voice agent">
    After recording, you'll interact with Cinemachine's voice agent powered by Gemini Live API. The agent will ask questions about your story:
    
    **Agent:** "What's happening in this scene?"
    
    **You:** "My dinosaur is exploring a magical forest looking for treasure."
    
    **Agent:** "What kind of forest? Dark and mysterious, or bright and colorful?"
    
    **You:** "A bright forest with tall trees and glowing flowers."
    
    The voice agent uses this conversation to understand what kind of fantasy background to generate and how to bring your toy to life.
    
    <Tip>
      Be descriptive! The more details you provide, the better the generated background will match your imagination.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Watch the magic happen">
    Cinemachine now processes your video through its AI pipeline:
    
    1. **Toy segmentation** — SAM2 separates your toy from hands and background
    2. **Background generation** — Nanobanana Pro creates the fantasy world you described
    3. **Character animation** — Eyes and mouth are added to your toy
    4. **Lip sync** — Mouth movements sync to your dialogue using Wav2Lip
    5. **Final composite** — Everything combines into your finished movie
    
    Processing typically takes a few minutes depending on scene length.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review your animated movie">
    Once processing completes, you'll see your transformed video:
    
    - Your toy performing against the fantasy background
    - No hands or home background visible
    - Expressive eyes that bring the character to life
    - Lip-synced mouth movements matching the dialogue
    - Subtle animations that make the toy feel alive
    
    <Check>
      Congratulations! You've created your first Cinemachine movie. Now try creating more scenes to build a complete story.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My toy isn't being detected properly">
    Make sure:
    - The toy contrasts well with the background
    - Lighting is even across the play area
    - You captured a clean reference frame without the toy
    - Your hands aren't completely covering the toy
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="The background doesn't match what I described">
    Try being more specific with the voice agent. Instead of "a forest," describe "a bright green forest with tall oak trees and purple flowers." The more details you provide, the better the result.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Processing is taking too long">
    Processing time depends on video length and resolution. For your first attempts, keep scenes under 60 seconds. You can create longer movies by combining multiple shorter scenes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
