## General questions

<Accordion title="How does Cinemachine work?">
Cinemachine uses a fixed camera setup where kids record themselves playing with toys and speaking dialogue. The AI system then:

1. Segments and extracts only the toy from each frame
2. Removes hands and the original background completely
3. Generates a fantasy background based on the story context
4. Adds animated eyes and mouth to the toy
5. Syncs the mouth movement to the recorded dialogue
6. Applies subtle animations to bring the toy to life

The result is a polished animated movie where toys come alive in imaginative worlds.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What kind of toys work best?">
Cinemachine works best with:

- **Action figures and dolls** - Clear body shapes that are easy to segment
- **Plush toys** - Soft toys with distinct colors and textures
- **Character toys** - Toys with recognizable faces or head areas
- **Single-color or high-contrast toys** - Easier for the AI to separate from hands

Toys with very small parts or transparent elements may be more challenging for the segmentation system to track accurately.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do I need special equipment?">
The basic requirements are:

- A **fixed camera setup** (smartphone, webcam, or camera on a tripod)
- Good lighting to help the AI distinguish the toy from hands
- A microphone to record dialogue (built-in device mic works fine)
- A clear reference frame of the empty scene before recording

No green screen or professional equipment is needed - the AI generates backgrounds from scratch.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does video processing take?">
Processing time depends on:

- **Video length** - Longer videos take more time to process
- **Complexity** - Multiple toys or rapid movements may increase processing time
- **Frame rate** - Higher frame rates require more processing

Typically, a 1-2 minute toy play session can be processed into a finished movie. The exact timing will vary based on your system and the complexity of the scene.
</Accordion>

## Features and capabilities

<Accordion title="Can I use multiple toys in one scene?">
Yes! Cinemachine can handle multiple toys in the same scene. The segmentation system tracks each toy individually, allowing you to create stories with multiple characters interacting together.

For best results, make sure each toy has distinct colors or shapes to help the AI differentiate between them.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How are backgrounds created?">
Backgrounds are generated using AI image generation based on the story context:

1. During recording, you describe the setting through voice interaction ("forest", "castle", "space", etc.)
2. The system generates a fantasy background matching your description
3. The background is created once per scene and remains static
4. Toys are composited onto the new background, replacing the original home environment

This means no green screen is required - any environment can become any imaginative world.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens to the audio?">
The audio handling includes:

- Your child's voice dialogue is preserved as recorded
- Lip sync is automatically applied to match the toy's mouth to the spoken words
- The system uses the audio to determine when characters are speaking
- Subtle body animations can be triggered by audio energy (toys bounce when speaking)

The original dialogue remains authentic to your child's storytelling.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How are eyes and mouths added to toys?">
The system adds animated features to bring toys to life:

- You can specify where the toy's "face area" is located (or the AI can attempt to detect it)
- Eyes and mouth graphics are generated to match the toy's aesthetic
- Features are tracked and positioned correctly as the toy moves through the scene
- The mouth is lip-synced to the recorded dialogue
- Eyes can blink and express emotion

The generated features blend with the toy's existing appearance.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What kind of animations are applied?">
Cinemachine adds subtle animations to make toys feel alive:

- **Squash and stretch** - Slight deformation for dynamic movement
- **Breathing motion** - Gentle rise and fall to suggest life
- **Head tilt** - Small rotations for expressiveness  
- **Bounce effects** - Movement synchronized with audio energy
- **Lip sync** - Mouth movements matching dialogue

These animations are mild and natural, enhancing the toy's performance without overwhelming it.
</Accordion>

## Creative possibilities

<Accordion title="What kinds of stories can kids create?">
Cinemachine supports any story kids can imagine:

- **Adventure tales** - Heroes on quests in fantasy worlds
- **Everyday scenarios** - Toys having conversations and solving problems
- **Fantasy epics** - Dragons, castles, magical forests
- **Space adventures** - Astronauts and aliens exploring new worlds
- **Educational content** - Toys teaching concepts through play

The only limit is imagination - the AI adapts backgrounds and animation to match the story being told.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long should a typical movie be?">
There's flexibility in movie length, but consider:

- **Short scenes (30-60 seconds)** - Perfect for young children's attention spans
- **Medium stories (1-3 minutes)** - Good for complete narrative arcs
- **Longer projects** - Can be broken into multiple scenes and combined

Shorter segments are easier to record, process faster, and are more manageable for beginning storytellers.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can scenes be edited or combined?">
Yes! The Cinemachine workflow supports:

- Recording multiple separate scenes
- Processing each scene individually
- Combining processed scenes into a longer movie
- Changing backgrounds between scenes for different settings

This allows kids to build complex stories scene-by-scene, just like real filmmaking.
</Accordion>
