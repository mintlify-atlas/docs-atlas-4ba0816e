## Segmentation issues

### Toy segmentation is incomplete or inaccurate

**Problem:** The AI isn't cleanly separating the toy from the background or hands.

**Solutions:**

<Tip>
**Improve lighting** - Ensure the toy is well-lit with even lighting. Shadows and dark areas make segmentation harder.
</Tip>

- **Use high-contrast toys** - Toys that contrast sharply with skin tone are easier to segment
- **Check the reference frame** - Make sure you captured a clean empty scene without the toy
- **Avoid rapid movements** - Slower, deliberate toy movements help the tracking system
- **Choose solid toys** - Toys with clear, solid bodies work better than those with many small parts

### Fingers appear in the final video

**Problem:** Parts of hands are showing up in the processed movie.

<Warning>
This typically happens at hand-toy contact points where fingers overlap the toy body.
</Warning>

**Solutions:**

- **Adjust grip** - Hold the toy from behind or underneath where it's less visible
- **Use mask refinement** - The system can apply mask erosion at hand-toy boundaries
- **Re-record the scene** - Sometimes repositioning your hand placement resolves the issue
- **Choose larger toys** - Bigger toys provide more surface area away from hand contact points

### Multiple toys aren't tracked correctly

**Problem:** When using multiple toys, the system confuses them or loses tracking.

**Solutions:**

- **Use distinctly different toys** - Vary colors, sizes, and shapes between toys
- **Avoid overlapping toys** - Keep some space between characters when they interact
- **Move one toy at a time** - For complex scenes, focus movement on one character per moment
- **Specify toys at the start** - Clearly identify each toy in the voice agent setup

## Background and compositing

### Generated background doesn't match expectations

**Problem:** The AI-generated fantasy background isn't what you envisioned.

**Solutions:**

<Tip>
**Be specific with descriptions** - Instead of "outside", try "sunny forest with tall trees" or "sandy desert with pyramids".
</Tip>

- **Provide story context** - Share more details through the voice agent about the scene's mood and setting
- **Request regeneration** - You can ask for a new background if the first doesn't fit
- **Use consistent terminology** - Stick to clear, standard setting descriptions (castle, forest, space, ocean, etc.)

### Toy edges look harsh against the background

**Problem:** The composited toy has hard edges that don't blend naturally.

**Solutions:**

- **Check lighting** - Match your recording lighting to the intended scene mood
- **Verify segmentation quality** - Poor segmentation creates rough edges
- **Consider toy texture** - Very fuzzy or textured toys may need special handling
- **Review the reference frame** - Ensure it's truly empty and matches recording conditions

### Background appears inconsistent across frames

**Problem:** The background seems to shift or change between frames.

<Warning>
Backgrounds should be generated once per scene and remain static. If you're seeing changes, there may be a processing issue.
</Warning>

**Solutions:**

- **Verify scene boundaries** - Make sure the system knows which frames belong to the same scene
- **Use one setting per recording** - Don't switch locations mid-recording
- **Check for processing errors** - Review logs for background generation issues

## Animation and lip sync

### Lip sync doesn't match dialogue

**Problem:** The toy's mouth movements are out of sync with the audio.

**Solutions:**

- **Check audio quality** - Clear, crisp dialogue helps the lip sync algorithm
- **Speak at moderate pace** - Very fast or mumbled speech is harder to sync
- **Position mouth correctly** - Ensure the mouth placement on the toy face is accurate
- **Verify audio sync** - Make sure the original audio hasn't drifted from the video

<Tip>
Record in a quiet environment to help the system accurately detect speech patterns.
</Tip>

### Eyes or mouth appear in wrong position on toy

**Problem:** Animated features don't line up with where they should be on the toy.

**Solutions:**

- **Manually specify face area** - Use the voice agent to tell the system where the face should be
- **Choose toys with clear faces** - Toys with defined head shapes work better
- **Keep toy orientation consistent** - Avoid flipping or rotating the toy dramatically
- **Review tracking** - Check that the toy position tracking is accurate throughout

### Animations look unnatural or too extreme

**Problem:** Squash, stretch, or bounce effects are too pronounced.

**Solutions:**

- **Adjust animation intensity** - Parameters can be tuned for more subtle effects
- **Match toy type** - Rigid toys should have less deformation than soft plush toys
- **Review movement speed** - Very fast toy movements may trigger exaggerated responses

## Recording and setup

### Camera moves during recording

**Problem:** The fixed camera shifted, invalidating the reference frame.

<Warning>
The fixed camera approach requires that the camera stays completely still throughout recording.
</Warning>

**Solutions:**

- **Use a stable mount** - Tripod, phone stand, or secure positioning
- **Mark the camera position** - Use tape to mark tripod leg positions
- **Avoid bumping** - Keep the recording area clear of obstacles
- **Re-record with new reference** - If the camera moved, capture a new reference frame and re-record

### Poor video quality affects processing

**Problem:** Blurry, dark, or low-resolution video creates processing issues.

**Solutions:**

- **Improve lighting** - Use natural light or add lamps to brighten the scene
- **Check camera settings** - Use the highest quality your device supports
- **Clean the lens** - Smudges and dirt reduce clarity
- **Avoid backlighting** - Don't position toys in front of bright windows
- **Use adequate resolution** - Higher resolution helps segmentation accuracy

### Reference frame includes unwanted objects

**Problem:** The "empty scene" reference frame has items in it.

**Solutions:**

- **Clear the entire scene** - Remove all toys, hands, and objects before capturing reference
- **Verify the reference** - Review the reference frame before starting to record
- **Retake if needed** - A clean reference frame is critical for good results

## Performance

### Processing takes too long

**Problem:** Video processing is slower than expected.

**Solutions:**

<Tip>
**Record shorter scenes** - Break your story into 30-60 second segments for faster processing.
</Tip>

- **Reduce frame rate** - Lower frame rates process faster while maintaining quality
- **Simplify the scene** - Fewer toys and simpler movements reduce processing time
- **Lower output resolution** - If appropriate, reduce the final video resolution
- **Process during downtime** - Start processing before breaks or overnight

### System runs out of memory

**Problem:** Processing fails due to insufficient memory.

**Solutions:**

- **Close other applications** - Free up system resources
- **Process shorter clips** - Break long videos into smaller segments
- **Reduce video resolution** - Lower resolution requires less memory
- **Check system requirements** - Ensure your device meets minimum specifications
