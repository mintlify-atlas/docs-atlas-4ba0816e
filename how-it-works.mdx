## The Cinemachine pipeline

Cinemachine uses a sophisticated AI pipeline to transform raw video of toy play into polished animated movies. Here's how each step works, explained in plain language.

## Fixed camera foundation

The entire system is built on a simple but powerful insight: **with a fixed camera, only the toys move**.

This seemingly simple choice eliminates the hardest technical challenge in video editing. Instead of trying to remove hands from a scene while preserving the original background, Cinemachine:

- Segments only the toy and discards everything else (hands, background, furniture)
- Generates a brand new fantasy background from scratch
- Composites the clean toy cutout onto the new world

<Info>
  This fixed camera approach means no complex hand inpainting, no background preservation issues, and cleaner results overall.
</Info>

## Pipeline stages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Video recording">
    The process starts when a child records themselves playing with a toy using a fixed camera. Before recording begins, Cinemachine captures a **reference frame** of the empty scene.
    
    **What happens:**
    - Fixed camera captures video at consistent framing
    - Child holds and moves toy while speaking dialogue
    - Hands appear in frame (this is expected and fine)
    - Audio is recorded simultaneously
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Toy segmentation">
    Cinemachine uses **SAM2** (Segment Anything Model 2) to identify and track the toy across all video frames.
    
    **What happens:**
    - SAM2 creates a precise mask of just the toy pixels
    - Hands touching the toy are excluded from the mask
    - The toy is tracked frame-by-frame as it moves
    - Clean toy cutout is extracted (no hands, no background)
    
    **Why this works:** The reference frame helps the system know what's background and what's new (the toy). SAM2's advanced segmentation can distinguish between the toy and hands, even at contact points.
    
    <Note>
      SAM2 is trained on millions of images and can accurately separate objects even in complex scenes. It understands the difference between a hand holding something and the object itself.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Story understanding with voice agent">
    A voice-to-voice conversation with the child determines what kind of world to create. This uses the **Gemini Live API** for natural, real-time conversation.
    
    **What happens:**
    - Child describes the story and setting through voice
    - AI agent asks clarifying questions
    - System builds context: "forest setting," "magical theme," "daytime," etc.
    - This context drives the next generation step
    
    **Example conversation:**
    - Agent: "Where is this adventure taking place?"
    - Child: "In a castle!"
    - Agent: "What kind of castle? Old and spooky or grand and royal?"
    - Child: "A grand royal castle with towers and flags!"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fantasy background generation">
    Based on the story context, Cinemachine generates a fantasy background using **Nanobanana Pro**, an AI image generation system.
    
    **What happens:**
    - Story context is converted to an image generation prompt
    - Nanobanana Pro generates a high-quality background scene
    - One background per scene (static, no temporal issues)
    - Background matches the described setting and mood
    
    <Tip>
      Because the background is generated once per scene and remains static, there are no temporal consistency issues or flickering between frames.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Character animation: Eyes and mouth">
    To bring the toy to life, Cinemachine adds expressive features:
    
    **Eyes:**
    - Generated to match the toy's aesthetic
    - Overlaid on the toy at the "face" position
    - Track with the toy as it moves through frames
    
    **Mouth with lip sync:**
    - Lip sync generated using **Wav2Lip** or **SadTalker**
    - Mouth movements match the recorded dialogue
    - Synced frame-by-frame to the audio
    
    **Subtle body animation:**
    - Slight squash/stretch effects
    - Breathing motion
    - Bounces timed to speech energy
    
    <Info>
      These animations are subtle but powerful—they transform a static toy cutout into a character that feels alive and expressive.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Final composite and movie output">
    All elements are combined into the final animated movie:
    
    **Composition layers:**
    1. Fantasy background (generated)
    2. Toy cutout (segmented, tracked position)
    3. Eyes overlay (tracking toy position)
    4. Lip-synced mouth (tracking toy position)
    5. Original audio (child's voice as dialogue)
    
    **Final output:**
    - Polished video with toy performing in fantasy world
    - No hands or original background visible
    - Expressive character animation
    - Professional-looking animated movie
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Technology stack

Here's what powers Cinemachine under the hood:

| Component | Technology | Purpose |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Voice agent | Gemini Live API | Real-time voice conversation to understand story |
| Toy segmentation | SAM2 | Separate toy from hands and background |
| Background generation | Nanobanana Pro | Create fantasy worlds from text descriptions |
| Feature generation | Nanobanana | Generate eyes and expressive elements |
| Lip sync | Wav2Lip / SadTalker | Sync mouth movements to dialogue audio |
| Video processing | Optical flow, frame interpolation | Ensure smooth tracking and transitions |

## Why this approach works

Cinemachine's design makes difficult problems tractable:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Fixed camera = clean segmentation" icon="video">
    No camera movement means reliable background subtraction and precise toy tracking without motion blur complexity.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Generated backgrounds = no inpainting" icon="image">
    Creating new backgrounds is easier than trying to preserve and repair original backgrounds after removing hands.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Voice interaction = natural UX" icon="microphone">
    Kids describe stories naturally through conversation instead of complex UI forms.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Static BG per scene = temporal consistency" icon="clock">
    One background per scene avoids the flickering and inconsistency issues of per-frame generation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Advanced: Handling edge cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What if hands overlap the toy completely?">
    SAM2's segmentation is robust but not perfect. If hands completely cover the toy for extended periods, the tracking may lose the toy. Best practice: ensure the toy is visible in most frames, even if hands are touching it.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How does Cinemachine handle multiple toys?">
    The current pipeline focuses on single-character scenes for optimal quality. Multiple toy support is planned, where each toy would be segmented separately and can have independent animations and dialogue.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Where should eyes and mouth be placed?">
    The system can auto-detect likely face areas on toys, or children can specify through the voice agent ("The eyes go near the top" or "It's a dinosaur, so the mouth is at the front").
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What resolution and frame rate?">
    Processing works best with 1080p video at 24-30fps. Higher resolutions increase processing time but can improve final quality for detailed toys.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
