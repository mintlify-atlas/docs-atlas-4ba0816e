## Bringing toys to life

After segmenting your child's toy and compositing it onto a fantasy background, Cinemachine adds the magic that transforms a static toy into a living character: expressive eyes, a talking mouth, and subtle body movements that make the character feel alive.

<Card title="Character animation pipeline" icon="wand-magic-sparkles">
  Cinemachine applies three layers of animation: facial features (eyes and mouth), synchronized lip movement, and gentle body motion. Together, these create the illusion of a character truly present in the story.
</Card>

## Eyes and mouth overlay

### Feature generation

Using Nanobanana (the same image generation technology behind background creation), Cinemachine generates custom eyes and mouth designs that match your toy's aesthetic:

- Eyes that complement the toy's style (cartoon, realistic, fantastical)
- Mouth shapes appropriate for the character type
- Visual consistency with the toy's existing colors and textures

<Accordion title="Face area detection">
  The system needs to know where to place eyes and mouth on the toy. This can happen in two ways:
  
  - **Voice-guided**: Your child can tell the voice agent where the face should be ("the face is at the top" or "the head is the round part")
  - **Auto-detection**: The system analyzes the toy's shape to identify likely face areas
  
  This remains an open design question in the current implementation.
</Accordion>

### Position tracking

As the toy moves through each frame, Cinemachine tracks where to place the facial features:

1. The toy's position and orientation are calculated from segmentation data
2. Eyes and mouth overlay positions are updated frame-by-frame
3. Features stay properly aligned even as the toy rotates or moves

<Info>
  The overlay system ensures facial features move naturally with the toy, maintaining the illusion that they're part of the toy itself rather than floating elements.
</Info>

## Lip sync to audio

One of the most engaging features is mouth movement that matches your child's recorded dialogue.

### Audio-driven mouth shapes

Cinemachine uses lip sync technology (similar to Wav2Lip or SadTalker) to:

- Analyze the audio waveform and phonemes (speech sounds)
- Generate appropriate mouth shapes for each sound
- Sequence mouth movements to match the timing of speech
- Blend mouth shapes smoothly for natural transitions

### Synchronized dialogue

The lip sync system processes your child's voice recording and creates mouth animations that:

- Open and close in rhythm with speech
- Form shapes corresponding to different sounds ("oo" vs "ee" vs "ah")
- Stay closed during silence
- React to audio energy (bigger movements for louder speech)

<Note>
  The current implementation keeps your child's original voice as the character's voice, preserving the authentic play experience. Whether to use generated character voices remains an open question.
</Note>

## Mild body animation

Beyond facial animation, Cinemachine adds subtle body movements that make the toy feel alive without overwhelming the scene.

### Squash and stretch

Borrowed from classic animation principles, squash and stretch adds organic feel:

- Slight vertical compression when the toy "lands" or settles
- Gentle elongation during upward movement
- Subtle deformation that maintains volume

<Warning>
  These effects are intentionally mild — the goal is to enhance believability, not to create exaggerated cartoon physics that might distract from the story.
</Warning>

### Breathing motion

Even when the toy is stationary, tiny movements suggest life:

- Gentle expansion and contraction mimicking breathing
- Slow, rhythmic motion at a natural breathing pace
- Subtle enough to be subconscious but effective at preventing a "frozen" look

### Audio-reactive animation

Body animation can respond to the character's voice:

- **Bounce on speech**: Slight movement timed to speech energy
- **Head tilt**: Minor rotation during emphasis or questions
- **Gesture hints**: Small movements that suggest the character is actively speaking

<Accordion title="How audio-reactive animation works">
  The system analyzes the audio waveform to detect speech energy and emphasis. When the character speaks louder or with more emotion, the body animation becomes slightly more pronounced. This creates a natural connection between what the character says and how it moves.
</Accordion>

## Technical approach

### Frame-by-frame overlay

Eyes, mouth, and body effects are applied to each frame:

```
Segmented Toy Cutout
        +
Generated Eyes (tracked position)
        +
Lip-synced Mouth (tracked + animated)
        +
Body Deformation (squash/stretch/breathing)
        =
Animated Character Frame
```

### Optical flow for smoothness

To ensure animations don't jitter or jump, Cinemachine uses:

- Optical flow tracking for smooth feature placement
- Frame interpolation for fluid motion
- Temporal smoothing to reduce frame-to-frame variance

## The final result

With all animation layers combined, your child's toy becomes a fully realized character:

- Expressive eyes that match the toy's personality
- A mouth that speaks in sync with dialogue
- Subtle body movements that suggest life and presence
- Smooth, professional-looking animation throughout

The combination of these techniques transforms raw play footage into a polished animated movie where toys truly come to life.
