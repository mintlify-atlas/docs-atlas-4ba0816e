## Conversational storytelling

Cinemachine uses voice-to-voice interaction to understand your child's creative vision. Rather than filling out forms or selecting from menus, kids simply talk to the system about their story — what's happening, where it takes place, and what they want to create.

<Card title="Powered by Gemini Live" icon="microphone">
  Cinemachine integrates the Gemini Live API for real-time, natural voice conversations. The system understands context, asks clarifying questions, and helps shape your child's ideas into a finished movie.
</Card>

## How it works

### Gemini Live API integration

The Gemini Live API provides real-time voice interaction capabilities:

- **Voice-to-voice**: Kids speak naturally and receive spoken responses
- **Real-time processing**: No waiting between question and answer
- **Contextual understanding**: The system remembers what was said earlier
- **Natural dialogue flow**: Conversations feel organic, not scripted

<Info>
  Unlike traditional voice assistants that process one command at a time, Gemini Live maintains conversational context throughout the entire movie creation process.
</Info>

### Understanding story context

Through conversation, the voice agent gathers crucial information:

**Setting and world**
- Where does the story take place? (forest, castle, space, etc.)
- What's the mood? (adventurous, mysterious, cheerful)
- What time of day is it?

**Character details**
- Who is the toy supposed to be?
- What personality does it have?
- How should it sound and move?

**Narrative flow**
- What happens in the story?
- When do scenes change?
- What's the beginning, middle, and end?

<Accordion title="Example conversation flow">
  **System**: "Hi! I'm excited to help you make a movie with your toy. What kind of adventure are we creating today?"
  
  **Child**: "My dinosaur is going to explore a jungle and find treasure!"
  
  **System**: "A jungle adventure — awesome! Is it a bright, sunny jungle or a dark, mysterious one?"
  
  **Child**: "It's sunny and there are lots of big trees."
  
  **System**: "Perfect! And when your dinosaur finds the treasure, what happens next?"
  
  This natural back-and-forth helps the system understand exactly what to generate.
</Accordion>

## Contextual intelligence

The voice agent doesn't just collect information — it understands how different elements connect:

### Scene detection

By listening to your child's story, the system identifies when scenes change:

- "First they're in the jungle, then they go into a cave"
- "After that, they fly to the castle"
- "At night, they go to sleep under the stars"

Each scene change triggers a new background generation, creating visual chapters in the story.

### Feature placement guidance

The voice agent can ask about and remember character details:

- "Where should the eyes be on your dinosaur?"
- "Does your character have a big mouth or a small mouth?"
- "Should the face be at the top or in the middle?"

<Note>
  Whether face placement is voice-guided or auto-detected remains an open design question, but the conversational interface makes it easy to gather this information when needed.
</Note>

### Iterative refinement

If something isn't clear, the voice agent asks follow-up questions:

- "You mentioned a castle — is it a big stone castle or a magical floating one?"
- "Should the forest have any special features like waterfalls or flowers?"
- "Is your character brave or scared in this scene?"

## Technical implementation

### Real-time voice processing

The Gemini Live API handles:

1. **Speech recognition**: Converting your child's words to text
2. **Natural language understanding**: Extracting meaning and intent
3. **Context management**: Maintaining conversation history
4. **Response generation**: Creating appropriate, context-aware replies
5. **Text-to-speech**: Speaking responses in a natural voice

### Integration with the pipeline

Information from voice conversations directly drives other systems:

```
Voice Conversation
        │
        ├──> Background Generation (setting details)
        ├──> Character Animation (personality traits)
        ├──> Scene Segmentation (narrative structure)
        └──> Feature Placement (face location, style)
```

<Warning>
  **Privacy note**: Voice interactions should be processed with appropriate privacy protections. Parents should understand how voice data is used and stored in the system.
</Warning>

## Benefits for creative expression

Voice interaction makes Cinemachine accessible to young children who:

- Can't type or use complex software interfaces
- Think in stories, not technical parameters
- Want to focus on creativity, not configuration
- Benefit from guided prompts and questions

### Natural for kids

Young children are already experts at voice interaction — they talk all the time during play. Cinemachine meets them where they are, turning play narration into movie direction.

### Guided creativity

The voice agent acts as a creative partner:

- Suggests ideas when kids are stuck
- Asks questions that spark imagination
- Helps structure stories with beginning, middle, and end
- Validates and encourages creative choices

<Card title="Learning through storytelling" icon="book">
  The conversational process itself is educational. Kids practice narrative structure, descriptive language, and creative problem-solving — all while thinking they're just playing and making a movie.
</Card>

## Open questions

The voice interaction system continues to evolve:

- **Voice preservation**: Should the final movie use your child's original voice, or generate character voices?
- **Multi-character scenes**: How should the conversation handle multiple toys in one scene?
- **Director mode**: Could kids give real-time direction during recording ("now pan left", "zoom in")?
- **Collaboration**: Could multiple kids work on one story together through voice?

## The user experience

From a parent's perspective, voice interaction means:

- No learning curve for software interfaces
- Natural integration with how kids already play
- Minimal screen time (kids talk instead of tapping)
- Documentation of storytelling and language development

From a child's perspective:

- Talking to a friendly creative assistant
- Being heard and understood
- Seeing their ideas come to life
- Making real movies like the "grown-ups" do

Voice interaction is the bridge between imagination and creation — the invisible interface that makes Cinemachine feel like magic.
